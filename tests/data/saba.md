# Saba

Saba is a Caribbean island and special municipality of the Netherlands. It lies in the northern Leeward Islands and is the smallest populated island of the former Netherlands Antilles. With a population of roughly 2,000 people, it is one of the least densely populated islands in the Caribbean.

**Geography**

The island has a land area of about 13 square kilometres and is dominated by Mount Scenery, a dormant volcano and the highest point in the Kingdom of the Netherlands. The main settlement is The Bottom. The terrain is steep and rugged, with lush tropical rainforest covering the upper slopes of the volcano and dramatic cliffs dropping into the sea along much of the coastline.

**Culture and access**

Saba is known for diving, hiking, and a quiet, small-scale way of life. There is no significant beach; the island is reached by boat or small aircraft. English and Dutch are both used. The island's Juancho E. Yrausquin Airport features one of the shortest commercial runways in the world, adding to Saba's reputation as a remote and unique destination.
