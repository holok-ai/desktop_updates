# St. Croix

St. Croix is the largest of the United States Virgin Islands, in the Caribbean Sea. It lies south of St. Thomas and St. John and is a U.S. territory.

**Geography and towns**

The island has an area of about 84 square miles. The main towns are Christiansted and Frederiksted. The terrain includes hills, rainforest, and coastal areas.

**History and economy**

St. Croix has a long history of sugarcane and rum production. Today the economy relies on tourism, rum distilling, and some light industry. The island is known for diving, historic Danish colonial architecture, and beaches.
